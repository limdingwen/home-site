Passive vs Active Devices

The definition of passive and active devices, in electronic terms, is that an active component can output more energy than inputted, while a passive component cannot. That means that computers are active, while typewriters and lightbulbs are passive. Practically, and in this log, I define passive devices as things you can leave untouched for 20 years and can still be used, while with active, you cannot.

Pros and cons of a passive device

Pros: Doesn't need energy to function, can last a long time, generally reliable
Cons: Limited in functionality, limited in accuracy and precision (practically), slow and tedious to use

Pros and cons of an active device

Pros: Wide range of functionality, easier to make more accurate and precise measurements, faster and less tedious to use
Cons: Requires energy, requires recharging, generally less reliable

Conclusion

I use a bit of both. Paper and pen for quick notes and doodles, paper and a printer for manuals, paper books, video games, watches. Maybe someday a sundial just in case my watch runs out of battery. ♠
